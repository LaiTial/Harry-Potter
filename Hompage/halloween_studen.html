History of Quidditch

Quidditch is a favorite sport for wizards. Wizards in Europe have been using broomsticks as transportation since about 962 A.D. and enjoyed many kinds of broomsticks. Quidditch, among other things, developed the broom race he enjoyed in the Quireditch Marsh region of England around the 11th century. At first, he put a ball made of leather (quaffle) into the opponent's tree and threw rocks to drop the opponent's player. The rock developed into a ball called the (Bludger) after it spread widely over 100 years. From the mid 13th century, a new element was added to Quidditch: "Golden Snitch" and "Today's Scout." At first, a small bird named "Golden Snippet" was released to the stadium to kill Snippet and finish the game. However, there has been a growing criticism for protecting Snippet, and later a wizard named Bowman Wright invented the ball called Golden Snitch as a substitute for Snippet.

how to play

A total of four balls are used for Quidditch. Quaffle, Bludger, and Golden Snitch. The quaffle is the largest red 30 cm leather ball, and is a goal ball. You can score 10 points if you put a quay into the net. Use two Bludger, which is black, 25 cm steel balls. He moves himself and runs toward the nearest player and drops the player. Usually it ends with an injury, but in the worst case, there is a risk of death. In order to prevent his opponent from getting hurt, beaters must use bat to hit Blaser against their opponents. Golden Snitch is the smallest 2.5 cm ball in gold. It is called Snitch for short, and it is a golden nut-shaped ball with wings. It is difficult to catch the ball because it is so fast, and since the game ends by scoring 150 points when a seeker catches the ball, it is the most important ball that determines the win or loss. Usually, Snitch's catch wins, but sometimes it isn't. They also wear gloves to make the first player to remember the skin.
A total of 14 players will participate in each team. Chaser picks up the quaffle and throws it into the opponent's net for scoring. Three players will play. Beater is the role of clubbing Bludger to the other side to prevent the bat from harming his side. Two players will play. Keeper is responsible for keeping three goals and preventing the opponent from adding quaffles. One player will play. Seeker plays the role of finding and catching Snitch, and the game ends only when a hunter catches Snitch.The broomstick will do as well as possible. One player will play. Because it is the most important role, it is likely to be targeted by opponents.

the rules of the game

  • When the referee enters the center circle with four balls, 14 players are waiting in a broomstick.
  • When the ball is released out of the box, the players jump straight to the sky and the game begins.
  • If you commit a foul, your opponent will be given a free throw.
  • It is 10 points when you add the quaffle.
  • When a seeker catches Snitch, he scores 150 points and the game ends.
  • The game will last forever if the seeker cannot catch Snitch. The maximum record is said to be three months.
